Places To Visit in Istanbul

Istanbul , Turkey's cultural and historical heart,is a captivating blend of ancient wonders and modern marvels. With its rich tapestry of civilizations, Istanbul boasts an unparalleled heritage that spans thousands of years. From the majestic Hagia Sophia and the stunning Blue Mosque to the bustling Grand Bazaar and the serene Bosphorus, every corner of the city exudes a sense of timeless beauty and intrigue. Its vibrant streets are alive with the echoes of empires past, while its breathtaking skyline offers a glimpse into the city's dynamic present. Whether wandering through the narrow alleys of the Old City or marveling at the glittering lights along the waterfront, Istanbul never fails to enchant with its unparalleled charm and allure.You will see my favorite places in Istanbul on this website.


KADIKOY

Kadıköy is a district located on the Anatolian side of Istanbul, known for its vibrant cultural scene. It's particularly popular among young people and hosts a variety of activities and entertainment venues. Places to visit in Kadıköy include Moda Beach, Kadıköy Bazaar, Osman Ağa Mosque, and Bahariye Avenue. Moda Beach is especially known for its beautiful scenery and walking promenade. Known as the "Bar Street," (My favorite place) Kadife Street is the heart of Kadıköy's nightlife. Here, you can find a variety of bars, pubs, and cafes with different styles. With live music, DJ performances, and cozy atmospheres, it offers visitors a chance to have an enjoyable night out. Kadıköy is famous for its energetic and colorful atmosphere.


BEYLERBEYI PLACE

Beylerbeyi Palace is a magnificent historic structure located in the Üsküdar district of Istanbul. It was commissioned by Sultan Abdülaziz in the late 19th century during the final years of the Ottoman Empire. Situated in a commanding position overlooking the Bosphorus, the palace boasts European-style architecture. While retaining traditional Ottoman palace features, Beylerbeyi Palace also incorporates influences from the West. It was constructed inspired by European palaces and equipped with the most modern technologies of the time. The palace's interior rooms are adorned with ornate furniture, splendid chandeliers, and rich decorations. Throughout history, Beylerbeyi Palace has hosted important state guests and served as a venue for significant diplomatic negotiations. Its gardens are also stunning, filled with the cool breezes of the Bosphorus. Now serving as a museum, the palace offers visitors the opportunity to explore the grandeur and rich history of the Ottoman Empire. Beylerbeyi Palace stands as an important part of Istanbul's cultural and historical heritage.

MAIDEN'S TOWER

The Maiden's Tower is a structure built on a small islet near Salacak, close to the entrance of the Istanbul Strait into the Sea of Marmara. It is a building surrounded by various legends and myths, and it has become a symbol of Üsküdar, the district it belongs to, being the only surviving monument from the Byzantine era. Originally serving as a burial place during the Greek period, this island was used as a customs station with an additional building constructed during the Byzantine period. In the Ottoman era, it served various functions, ranging from a show platform to a defense fortress, from an exile station to a quarantine room. Its primary function, guiding people with its presence and signaling passing ships with its beacon at night, has remained unchanged for centuries. Above all, the Maiden's Tower guides dreams from the past to the future. In the year 2000, the Maiden's Tower was restored and transformed into a venue where the sounds of cutlery and chatter can be heard. Access to the Maiden's Tower is provided by boats from Salacak and Ortaköy. With its ancient history, the Maiden's Tower was once used to collect taxes from ships passing through the strait. A large chain was drawn along the European coast from the tower, and ships were allowed to pass between the Asian coast and the Maiden's Tower (as the size of ships was small back then). After a while, the tower could not bear the weight of the chain and collapsed towards the European side. The ruins of the tower can still be seen underwater when looked at from the tower's base. Known as Arkla (small castle) and Damialis (calf) in ancient times, the tower once gained fame as the "Tour de Leandros" (Leandros' tower). Now, it is associated with the name Maiden's Tower and is referred to by this name.

GALATA TOWER

Galata Tower is an important historical structure located in the Galata district of Istanbul. It was initially built as "Christea Turris" by the Genoese in 1348. The tower was constructed for the defense of Galata, a Genoese colony. During that time, Istanbul was under the control of the Byzantine Empire, and Galata served as a Genoese trading center. The stones used in the construction of the tower were gathered from the ruins of ancient Byzantine structures. The tower has a rectangular plan and stands at a height of 66.90 meters. Considering the technology available at the time of its construction, it is an impressive edifice. Throughout history, Galata Tower has undergone several repairs, restorations, and changes in function. Besides being used for defense purposes, it has served as a lookout tower for fires, a lighthouse, an astronomy observation point, and even a prison. Today, Galata Tower is considered one of the symbols of Istanbul and attracts the attention of tourists who want to see the panoramic view of the city. It houses a café and observation terrace, offering visitors a magnificent vista.

EMINONU

  2. Hagia Sophia Museum: Located in Sultanahmet Square, Hagia Sophia is one of the most significant structures of the Byzantine era and currently operates as a museum.
  3. Basilica Cistern: Situated near Hagia Sophia, this ancient underground water cistern is one of Istanbul's captivating sites that attracts visitors.
  4. Grand Bazaar: Eminönü is close to the Grand Bazaar, one of the oldest covered markets in the world. With thousands of shops, it offers visitors an opportunity for unique shopping experiences.

    6. located near Eminönü and provides visitors with an enjoyable shopping experience.
  6. Sultanahmet Square and Topkapı Palace: Sultanahmet Square, an iconic landmark of Istanbul, hosts the historic Topkapı Palace, which served as the center of the Ottoman Empire.

ORTAKÖY

It is part of the Beşiktaş district and lies north of the Bosphorus. Throughout history, Ortaköy has carried traces of various civilizations. Historically, one of the most famous structures in Ortaköy is the Ortaköy Mosque, built in the 19th century. This mosque features a Baroque-style architecture and offers a magnificent view of the Bosphorus coast. Additionally, the district boasts historical buildings from the Ottoman era.
Ortaköy is one of the significant tourist areas in Istanbul. The Ortaköy Square is renowned for its restaurants, cafes, and handicraft vendors. Especially at night, the streets and square come alive and become a popular destination for both locals and tourists.
Ortaköy's location is noteworthy due to its proximity to other important districts of Istanbul. Situated close to Beşiktaş, Ortaköy is also on the northern side of the Bosphorus Bridge. Therefore, Ortaköy serves as a crucial crossing point connecting the European and Asian continents through the Bosphorus Bridge. Additionally, the district is near Dolmabahçe Palace, contributing to Istanbul's historical and cultural fabric.
